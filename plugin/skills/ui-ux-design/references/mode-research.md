# UI/UX – Research Mode

Understand users, flows, pain points, and business constraints. Define information architecture, primary journeys, and what the interface must make easy.

## Inputs

- feature requirements, PRDs, or a problem statement
- existing product, competitors, or domain benchmarks (optional)
- user-facing constraints (accessibility, localization, device targets)

## Process

1. **Understand the job** – What are users trying to accomplish? What currently gets in the way? What does success look like?
2. **Identify primary journeys** – the 2-5 flows that carry most of the product's value.
3. **Map information architecture** – the conceptual model the user needs to hold to navigate. Prefer shallow, legible hierarchies.
4. **Competitive/domain scan** (when useful) – 3-5 relevant products, noting what they get right and wrong for this job.
5. **Accessibility & platform constraints** – tap targets, contrast, focus states, safe areas, localization length, input modalities.

## Output

Return:
- **Job-to-be-done** – one-sentence summary of what the interface must make easy
- **Primary journeys** – 2-5 flows, each as an ordered list of user intents and system responses
- **IA sketch** – hierarchy/navigation model (bullet list or simple tree)
- **Constraints** – accessibility, platform, performance, localization
- **Open questions** – the few things that must be decided before design can proceed
